Oslo

1 Night · Arrival Day · June 27

Maaemo

3 Michelin Stars Can't Get in NYC
Creative New Nordic ~$500/person ~20 Courses Chef: Esben Holmboe Bang

Oslo's only 3-star restaurant. All produce is biodynamic, organic, or wild -- a pure exploration of Norwegian terroir. The experience begins in a cozy lounge before moving to a dramatic, high-ceilinged dining room with a theatrically lit open kitchen. The non-alcoholic juice pairing is genuinely exceptional -- some guests prefer it to the wine. A ~20-course surprise seasonal tasting menu that pushes the boundaries of Nordic cuisine.

Feasibility Note

First seating is 17:00. If arriving Oslo by early afternoon after a transatlantic flight, this is ambitious with jet lag. Consider this a "if we're feeling up for it" option. The experience runs 3-4 hours.

Dronning Eufemias gate 23, Oslo 0194 Book April 1 at noon CET Book on Tock →

Bergen

3 Nights · Days 3-5 · June 29 - July 1

Bergen's Michelin Boom

Bergen now has three Michelin-starred restaurants as of 2025 -- Lysverket (retained), plus two NEW stars: Gaptrast and Omakase by Sergey Pak. Combined with private-island dining at Cornelius and 800-year-old fish market traditions, Bergen is the culinary heavyweight of this trip.

Lysverket

1 Michelin Star
Modern Norwegian ~$180-230/person 10 Courses Chef: Christopher Haatuft

Located inside the KODE 4 art museum -- a stunning 1938 former power company HQ. Chef Christopher Haatuft was featured in NYT as "redefining Nordic cuisine." The 10-course tasting menu always includes handpicked scallops landed by the chef's close friend. He also runs MatTak, growing vegetables on Bergen rooftops. Wine, non-alcoholic, and bottle pairings available.

Rasmus Meyers alle 9, 5015 Bergen Book 4-6 weeks ahead lysverket.no →

Gaptrast

1 Michelin Star New 2025
Modern Nordic / Charcoal Fire $$$$ Surprise Menu Chef: Kristian Bretten Vangen

Earned a Michelin star within its FIRST YEAR of opening (opened 2024, starred 2025). The evening begins with local cider and a tour of dry-aged ingredients, then moves to a moody lounge for meticulously prepared snacks, then into the main restaurant with dark lighting focused on an open kitchen with a charcoal fire ablaze. Incredibly original creations -- think duck with winter apples and vin jaune. Wine list features classics plus exclusive local apple cider.

Central Bergen Book ASAP -- Bergen's hottest

Omakase by Sergey Pak

1 Michelin Star New 2025 Can't Get in NYC
Edomae Omakase $$$$ Only 10 Seats World Sushi Champion

A former world sushi champion serves omakase to just 10 guests at a time. Sergey Pak blends high-quality Norwegian ingredients (Bergen's incredible seafood) with impeccable Japanese Edomae techniques. His brother Vladimir also won the World Sushi Championship and holds a Michelin star. The 20-course version (Friday-Sunday 8pm seating) is the full experience. Norwegian seafood prepared with Japanese mastery -- unlike any omakase in NYC.

Skostredet Hotel, Bergen 10 seats -- book immediately

Cornelius Seafood Restaurant

Private Island Can't Get in NYC
Ultra-Fresh Seafood ~$137 incl. boat 25-min boat from Bryggen

One of Norway's finest seafood restaurants, literally on a private island. Board the restaurant's own boat from Bryggen for a 25-minute ride through the archipelago. "Meteorological menus" change daily based on what the sea delivers. Summer means dining on the quay with doors open. The boat ride IS part of the experience.

The Experience

Depart 18:00 from Dreggekaien quay, shed #8, opposite Radisson Blu Royal Hotel. Return 22:30. Email confirmation = your boat ticket.

Holmen island, west of Bjoroy Book 1-2 weeks ahead corneliusrestaurant.no →

Enhjorningen (The Unicorn)

Can't Get in NYC
Traditional Norwegian Seafood $$$ UNESCO Bryggen, est. 1300s

Housed in one of Bergen's OLDEST buildings, dating back to the 1300s. Original wood floors from the 1700s, coral-colored walls, antique furniture. Located right in the UNESCO World Heritage Bryggen wharf buildings.

Must-Try

Whale carpaccio (impossible in NYC!), brown cheese ice cream, wolf fish, and bacalau. The whale meat is uniquely Norwegian and legally unavailable in the United States.

Enhjorningsgarden 29, Bergen (UNESCO Bryggen) Book 1-2 weeks ahead

Iris at The Salmon Eye

Hardangerfjord · Day Trip from Bergen

Closure Notice -- January 2026

Iris at The Salmon Eye closed in January 2026. The restaurant had earned 1 Michelin Star in 2024, was named a TIME Magazine "World's Greatest Place," and served a groundbreaking 18-course menu to just 24 guests per day inside a floating steel sculpture in the Hardangerfjord. The concept may relaunch in a new form. Monitor thesalmoneye.com and restaurantiris.no for any 2026 announcements. If it reopens, it would be the single most unique dining experience on this trip.

Iris at The Salmon Eye

Closed Jan 2026 Was 1 Michelin Star Nowhere Else on Earth
18-Course Tasting Was ~$430/person 24 Guests Max Chef: Anika Madsen

What it was: A floating restaurant inside a mirrored steel sculpture clad in 9,250 fish-scale-shaped plates, anchored in the Hardangerfjord. Guests boarded an electric boat from Rosendal, stopped at Chef Anika Madsen's boathouse for snacks, then entered the 4-level structure for an underwater gallery experience, an 18-course tasting menu with fjord views, and a rooftop course grilled over open flame. The menu told the story of ocean sustainability using lumpsucker fish, carbon-neutral salmon, invasive sea urchin, and carbon-sequestering seaweed. The 6-hour experience was unreplicable anywhere on Earth.

What to Do Instead

If Iris does not reopen, use the Bergen day for the Cornelius private island dinner (similar boat-to-restaurant concept) or the Hardangerfjord scenic drive to Rosendal with lunch at a local fjord restaurant. The Salmon Eye structure itself may still be viewable from the water.

Salmon Eye, Hardangerfjord (boat from Rosendal) Monitor thesalmoneye.com →

Frantzen

3 Michelin Stars Can't Get in NYC
Nordic + Japanese ~$530/person ~10 Courses 23 Seats Only Chef: Bjorn Frantzen

Sweden's ONLY 3-star restaurant. Chef Bjorn Frantzen is the only chef in the world helming THREE separate 3-Michelin-star restaurants (Stockholm, Singapore, Dubai). The experience spans 3 floors of a Gamla Stan townhouse: lobby, dining room, and loft lounge. Ranked #6 World's 50 Best (2021), #1 La Liste (2023). Modern and creative with classic techniques including open fire. Both alcoholic and non-alcoholic pairings available.

Lilla Nygatan 21, Gamla Stan, Stockholm Book May 1 at 10:00 AM sharp restaurantfrantzen.com →

Ekstedt

1 Michelin Star Can't Get in NYC
All Fire Cooking ~$240/person 8 Courses Chef: Niklas Ekstedt

ZERO ELECTRICITY used in cooking. Everything -- birchwood, smoke, soot, ash, fire -- from 6 different fiery ovens. Inspired by Chef Ekstedt's childhood in the Arctic forests of Jamtland and 18th-century Swedish cookbooks from before electricity. Visit begins with a kitchen tour and signature oyster snack cooked in a cone over flames. Each of the 8 courses is treated with fire, wood, or smoke.

Standout Dishes

Birch-fired broad beans with yogurt and sturgeon caviar. Seaweed-fired langoustine with celeriac. Juniper-smoked reindeer saddle with beetroot and fig leaf.

Humlegardsgatan 17, 114 46 Stockholm Releases 6 weeks out at midnight ekstedt.nu →

Pelikan

Can't Get in NYC
Traditional Swedish Beer Hall $40-60/person Est. 1733 Cultural Heritage

An Art Nouveau classified cultural heritage beer hall with towering arched windows, high vaulted ceilings, and long communal tables. One of the few restaurants where aquavit is served with food year-round. The food is what Swedes USED to cook at home -- traditional husmanskost elevated. Drawings by regular artist "Slas" hang on the walls.

Must-Order

Kottbullar (Swedish meatballs) -- hand-rolled, consistently named the best in Stockholm. Multiple herring preparations. Grosshandlarmiddag ("Wholesaler's Dinner") for historical recipes. Pair with aquavit + Carnegiebryggeriet craft beer.

Blekingegatan 40, Sodermalm, Stockholm Book 1-2 weeks ahead pelikan.se →

Rosendals Tradgard

Can't Get in NYC
Biodynamic Garden Cafe $-$$ Djurgarden Island Non-Profit

A non-profit biodynamic garden and greenhouse cafe on Djurgarden. Vegetables grown on-site, bread from their own 16-ton woodfired oven (Stockholm's largest). Over 100 rose species, a vineyard producing biodynamic wine, apple orchards. All bread is sourdough. Opens 11:00 -- pair with an early Djurgarden walk and Fabrique coffee first.

Stockholm Fika Guide

The Swedish Art of Coffee & Pastry

What is Fika?

Fika is NOT "coffee break." It is a Swedish cultural institution -- a deliberate pause to connect with others over coffee and something sweet. Most Swedes do fika at least twice daily. Order a coffee, select a pastry (kanelbulle -- cardamom-scented cinnamon bun -- is the classic), sit down, and talk. Never fika alone if you can help it. The conversation IS the point.

Vete-Katten

Since 1928 · Kungsgatan 55 · 9 locations

THE iconic Stockholm fika institution. Stepping in feels like the 1920s -- a labyrinth of rooms with chandeliers and vintage decor. Founded by Ester Nordhammar (the name means "the wheat cat"). Famous for kanelbullar, princess cakes, and cardamom rolls. Always packed with locals.

Mon-Fri 7:30-19:30 4.4/5 (7,069 reviews) $

Cafe Saturnus

Eriksbergsgatan 6, Ostermalm

Known for OVERSIZED cinnamon buns that have become legendary. French-inspired decor with soft lighting. Upscale Ostermalm near Humlegarden Park. Also excellent for weekday breakfast (from 8am) -- omelettes, open sandwiches, salads.

Giant Kanelbullar French-Inspired $-$$

Fabrique

~19 locations · Royal Warrant Holder (2024)

Artisanal stone oven bakery. Purveyor to the Royal Court of Sweden. Their sourdough is legendary. Cinnamon buns, cardamom buns, saffron buns, croissants. Gamla Stan location opens 7:30 AM weekdays. Items sell out fast -- come before 10:30.

Mon-Fri 7:00-19:00 Royal Warrant $

Cafe Pascal

4 locations + Roastery · Award-Winning

Won Gulddraken for best cafe in Stockholm (2019). Chemex pour-overs, expertly crafted espressos. Breakfast: open sandwiches, croissants, Swedish buns, chia pudding. Industrial-chic vibes.

Mon-Thu 7:00-19:00 Specialty Coffee $-$$

The Star Pastry: Kanelbulle

Cardamom-scented cinnamon brioche sprinkled with pearl sugar. Popularized in the 1920s. Sweden has a national Kanelbullens Dag (Cinnamon Bun Day) on October 4. For the BEST version in Stockholm, the consensus is Vete-Katten for tradition and Cafe Saturnus for the most theatrical size.

Ferry Dining

Viking Line · Stockholm to Helsinki · Overnight

The Ferry IS the Experience

Departing ~16:30, arriving ~10:30 next morning. 17 hours through Stockholm and Finnish archipelagos. June = 11:30 PM sunsets visible from the deck. The smorgasbord dinner + sunrise breakfast pairing is the move. Book a window table for the sunset -- absolutely worth the small extra charge.

🍴

Smorgasbord Buffet

THE signature experience. Full Scandinavian smorgasbord -- curated Nordic delicacies, NOT a typical cruise buffet. ALL-INCLUSIVE: wine, beer, soft drinks, coffee, tea. Book in advance (sells out). Add a window table for the 11:30 PM sunset.

🍽

A La Carte Restaurant

More intimate, curated gourmet menu. Reservations recommended. A calmer alternative if the buffet energy feels like too much.

☕

Premium Breakfast

Included with Seaside Premium cabin. Cold meats, cheeses, fresh pastries, American-style pancakes, hot dishes to order. Book the Seaside Premium cabin for this.

🍸

Bar & Dancing

Nightly trivia, live bands, karaoke, light food. The quintessentially Scandinavian ferry party experience. Embrace it.

Helsinki

2 Nights · Days 10-11 · July 6-7

Finnjavel Salonki

1 Michelin Star Can't Get in NYC
Modern Finnish EUR 52 early / more for full 10 Tables Chef: Tommi Tuominen

The name means "Damn Finn" in Swedish -- a tongue-in-cheek nod to old stereotypes. A homage to Finnish cuisine: countryside and city Finns who cook, bake, hunt, and forage. Two concepts: Salonki (fine dining, 10 tables, every piece of cutlery made by Finnish craftspeople) and Sali (casual, elegant Finnish home cooking). Takes inspiration from Finnish school lunches, hangover cures, home cooking, and candy bars -- then elevates them to art. Vendace roe, pike, reindeer, malt pudding (mammi).

Best Service Award

Won Best Service in Nordic Countries (2021). The early Fri-Sat seating (15:00-18:00) offers a 3-course menu at EUR 52 -- exceptional value for Michelin dining.

Kunsthalle Helsinki, Nervanderinkatu 3 Book 2-4 weeks ahead finnjavel.fi →

Gron

1 Michelin Star Green Star
Plant-Focused Nordic EUR 168/person Only 7 Tables Chefs: Toni Kostian + Johan Kurkela

"Gron" means "green" in Swedish. The plant-based menu is every bit as ambitious as any meat-based one -- groundbreaking for vegetarians, but omnivores love it equally. Seasonal vegetables, wild herbs, responsibly sourced produce. The chefs forage across Finland. Open kitchen where chefs serve and explain each course. Many critics believe it deserves a second star. The Michelin Green Star recognizes their exemplary sustainability practices.

Albertinkatu 36, Helsinki Only 7 tables -- book early restaurantgron.com →

Savoy

Can't Get in NYC
Finnish-French ~EUR 100/person lunch Est. 1937 Chef: Helena Puolakka

Interior designed by Aino and Alvar Aalto in 1937. The famous Savoy Vase (Aalto Vase) was originally designed for this restaurant. Recently renovated by Ilse Crawford in cooperation with the Alvar Aalto Foundation. 8th-floor views over Esplanade Park and Helsinki Cathedral. One of Finland's largest wine cellars. The design heritage alone makes it unmissable.

8th floor, Etelaesplanadi 14, Helsinki Book 1-2 weeks ahead savoyhelsinki.fi →

Loyly

Sauna + Restaurant
Modern Finnish $$-$$$ Sauna EUR 26/2hr TIME 100 Greatest Places

Award-winning architecture. Four saunas including a glass-walled "sauna with a view" onto the Baltic Sea. The restaurant has an expansive terrace with Baltic views. Honest assessment: architecture and sauna are exceptional, but food reviews are mixed and it gets crowded in summer. Consider using Loyly purely for the sauna and dining elsewhere.

Hernesaarenranta 4, Helsinki Book sauna in advance loylyhelsinki.fi →

Markets & Street Food

Kauppatori (Market Square)

Harborside Outdoor Market

Fresh fried vendace (muikku) -- tiny fish fried whole in paper cones. Salmon soup, Karelian pies, fresh berries (July = peak!), reindeer sausage, coffee and pulla. Departure point for Suomenlinna ferry.

Harbor front, central Helsinki Walk-in

Vanha Kauppahalli (Old Market Hall)

Indoor Market · Since 1889

#1 must-try: Salmon soup at Soppakeittio (they only serve soup). Also: bear meat on bread (impossible in NYC!), reindeer soup, smoked salmon sandwiches, Karelian pies, L'Artisan Cafe croissants. ~EUR 10-15/meal.

Etelaranta, adjacent to Kauppatori Walk-in

Hakaniemi Market Hall

Most Authentic · 70 Shops · Since 1914

Helsinki's most traditional market -- where locals shop, not tourists. Reopened 2023 after renovation. Ekstrom's salmon soup + champagne, Cafe Katiska for fish pies, Nami for authentic Karelian pies, Panicuocoli for bio sourdough.

Hakaniemi, Helsinki Walk-in

Cafe Regatta

Tiny Red Cottage · Since 1887

A tiny red-and-green painted log cabin by the sea. One of Helsinki's rare surviving 19th-century wooden buildings. Buy sausages inside, GRILL them yourself on the outdoor fire. Korvapuusti, blueberry pie, Karelian pies. Opens 8 AM. 2 min from Sibelius Monument.

Merikannontie 8, Toolo, Helsinki Walk-in
