The Daylight Situation

In late June, all four cities get 18–20 hours of direct sunlight. There is no true darkness — just a brief twilight between sunset and sunrise. You'll want an eye mask for sleeping. The upside: you can hike at midnight, eat dinner on a terrace at 10 PM, and the golden hour light lasts for hours. Your body clock will shift — embrace it.

💳 Currency & Tipping

🇳🇴

Norway

Norwegian Krone (NOK)
~10 NOK = $1 USD
Tipping not expected. Round up at restaurants if service is exceptional. Card payment universal — many places don't accept cash.
🇸🇪

Sweden

Swedish Krona (SEK)
~10.5 SEK = $1 USD
Tipping not expected. 5–10% for exceptional service. Sweden is essentially cashless — some places literally cannot accept cash.
🇫🇮

Finland

Euro (EUR)
~€0.92 = $1 USD
Tipping not expected. Card payment universal. The Euro makes pricing easier to estimate for Americans.

Key Takeaway

Scandinavia is essentially cashless. Your US credit card with no foreign transaction fees will work everywhere. Apple Pay and contactless are universal. You do NOT need to exchange cash at the airport. The only place you might need a small amount of cash is the Kotiharjun Sauna in Helsinki (€14, some reports say cash preferred).

🚂 Getting Around

✈️

JFK → Oslo Gardermoen (OSL)

SAS nonstop, 7h 35m. Evening departure Jun 26, arrive morning Jun 27. Airport Express train (Flytoget) to Oslo Central Station: 19 minutes, every 10 min, ~$20.

Book at: flysas.com · Flytoget.no for airport train
🚂

Oslo → Bergen: Bergen Railway (Bergensbanen)

World's most scenic train, 6.5 hours. Departs Oslo S 8:25 AM, arrives Bergen ~2:30 PM. Crosses Hardangervidda plateau at 1,222m. Book window seat on right side facing west.

Book at: vy.no · ~$40–80 advance booking · Book 4–6 weeks ahead for summer window seats
🚗

Bergen + Fjord Country: Rental Car (1 day)

Rent a car for the Hardangerfjord/Flåmsbana day trip (Day 4). Bergen → fjord country → Bergen. ~80 km each way. Return car before flying to Stockholm.

Avis, Hertz, Sixt all at Bergen airport · Tolls automated via rental company
✈️

Bergen (BGO) → Stockholm Arlanda (ARN)

SAS or Norwegian, ~1.5 hr flight. Multiple daily morning departures. Arlanda Express train into Stockholm city center: 18 min, ~$30.

Book at: flysas.com or norwegian.com · Arlanda Express: arlandaexpress.com
🚶

Stockholm: On Foot + Metro + Ferry

No car needed. Gamla Stan, Östermalm, Södermalm all walkable. Metro for longer distances. Stromma ferries to archipelago islands. SL card covers metro, bus, tram, inner ferry.

SL Access card: sl.se · Archipelago ferries: stromma.com or waxholmsbolaget.se
⛴️

Stockholm → Helsinki: Overnight Ferry (Tallink Silja)

Tallink Silja departs Värtahamnen terminal ~16:30–16:45, arrives Helsinki Olympia Terminal ~09:10–10:30. 17 hours through Stockholm & Finnish archipelagos. Book Commodore Class cabin with balcony (Deck 11, Marimekko-themed, lounge access). Recommended over Viking Line for daily departures and more elegant atmosphere.

Book at: tallinksilja.com · Commodore cabins sell out for summer — book 2–3 months ahead
🚶

Helsinki: On Foot + Tram + Ferry

Very compact, walkable city. Tram for longer distances. HSL ferry to Suomenlinna (15 min from Market Square, every 20 min). Helsinki metro for southern districts.

HSL day ticket: hsl.fi · Covers tram, metro, bus, Suomenlinna ferry
✈️

Helsinki (HEL) → Copenhagen (CPH) → JFK: SAS via Copenhagen

SAS SK 705 Helsinki→Copenhagen, then SK 903 Copenhagen→JFK. Jul 9 departure. Helsinki-Vantaa airport: train from central station, 30 min. Connection at Copenhagen.

Book at: flysas.com · SK 705 + SK 903

🧳 Packing List

🏔 Fjord & Nature Days

  • Waterproof shell jacket (Bergen rain is real)
  • Merino wool mid-layer (12°C on fjord boats)
  • Comfortable hiking shoes/boots
  • Quick-dry pants (for kayaking days)
  • Daypack for fjord day trips
  • Sunglasses (20 hrs of light = bright)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50 (Nordic sun is deceptive)
  • Water bottle

🍽 Michelin & City Evenings

  • Smart casual outfit × 2 (Ekstedt, Lysverket)
  • Nice shoes/clean sneakers
  • Light blazer or jacket (evening dining)
  • No ties needed — Scandi smart casual is relaxed
  • Dark jeans work everywhere

🧖 Sauna Essentials

  • Swimsuit (for public saunas/Löyly)
  • Quick-dry towel (or rent at sauna)
  • Flip-flops/sandals
  • Note: Kotiharjun is traditional — optional nudity

😴 Sleep & Comfort

  • Sleep mask (CRITICAL — no darkness in June)
  • Ear plugs (ferry can be noisy)
  • Layers for sleeping (train/ferry temps vary)
  • Portable charger
  • EU plug adapter (Type C/F — same for all 3 countries)

Pack Light, Layer Smart

Scandinavians dress in layers, not bulky coats. A waterproof shell + merino mid-layer + t-shirt covers everything from 12°C fjord boats to 25°C Stockholm terraces. The same outfit works for both city walking and nature days. One plug adapter works in all three countries (EU Type C/F).

📱 Connectivity

📶 Best Option: eSIM

Get a European eSIM before departure. Airalo, Holafly, or Nomad all offer EU-wide data plans. Norway is not EU but most plans include it. 10GB for 2 weeks: ~$15–25. Activate before boarding at JFK. WiFi is excellent at all hotels and cafés.

🔄 Alternative: T-Mobile/AT&T

T-Mobile Magenta includes international data (slower speeds). AT&T International Day Pass: $12/day per line. Fine for basic maps/messaging. For heavy use (uploading photos, video calls), eSIM is better and cheaper.

Finnish Is Different

Norwegian and Swedish are closely related — they can mostly understand each other. Finnish is a completely unrelated language (Finno-Ugric, related to Estonian and Hungarian). It looks and sounds like nothing else in Europe. Don't even try to read signs — just enjoy the strangeness. Everyone under 60 speaks fluent English.

🛂 Entry & Border Crossings

🇪🇺 Schengen Area

All three countries are in the Schengen Area. You clear immigration once (at Oslo) and then move freely between Norway, Sweden, and Finland with no additional border controls. The overnight ferry crosses an international border — you won't even notice.

📋 ETIAS (Check Before Travel)

The EU's ETIAS travel authorization system may be active by summer 2026 for US citizens. Check etias.com or the EU official site 2–3 months before departure. If active, it's a simple online application (~€7, valid 3 years). This is NOT a visa — it's a pre-travel authorization similar to the US ESTA.

🇳🇴 Norway Note

Norway is NOT in the EU but IS in the Schengen Area. This means: Schengen entry rules apply (good), but Norway uses its own currency (NOK, not EUR) and is not covered by EU roaming regulations (check your eSIM includes Norway). Road tolls use the AutoPASS system — rental companies handle this automatically.

🍓 What's In Season

Peak Season (Available Everywhere)

  • Wild strawberries (smultron) — tiny, intense, served with cream
  • New potatoes — Scandinavian obsession, at every table
  • Wild Atlantic salmon — peak freshness, caught that morning
  • King crab — Norwegian Arctic, available in Bergen
  • Reindeer — stew, dried, tartare (all three countries)

Early Season (Just Starting)

  • Chanterelles — early July = season start in Finland/Sweden
  • Crayfish — Swedish season opens July 5 (just in time)
  • Cloudberries — late July start, may catch very early ones
  • White asparagus — end of season, last chance

Year-Round Nordic Specialties

  • Brown cheese (brunost) — sweet, caramel, goat's whey (Norway)
  • Karelian pies — rice-filled rye pastries + egg butter (Finland)
  • Gravlax — cured salmon with dill (all countries)
  • Kanelbullar — cinnamon buns, fika essential (Sweden)
  • Rye bread — dark, dense, different in each country
