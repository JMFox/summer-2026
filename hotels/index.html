The Thief

Art hotel on the Oslo Fjord · Gateway to the Bergen Railway
Address Landgangen 1, Tjuvholmen, Oslo
Rooms ~114 rooms & suites
Built 2013
Member Preferred Hotels & Resorts
Correction from earlier plans

The Thief has approximately 114 rooms, not 62 as previously listed. It is a mid-sized art hotel, not a tiny boutique. The quality and art integration remain exceptional regardless.

Why This One

Built on Tjuvholmen — "Thief Islet," once overrun by thieves and outcasts — The Thief carries its outlaw heritage into a luxury art hotel that cost US$1 million per room to build. Every corridor and room is filled with rotating contemporary art, including original works by Sir Peter Blake (of the Beatles' Sgt. Pepper album cover). The hotel is physically connected to the Astrup Fearnley Museum of Modern Art, with free entry for all guests.

This is Oslo's only hotel with direct fjord waterfront access. The Tjuvholmen neighborhood is the city's most vibrant arts, food, and waterfront district. For one night before catching the Bergen Railway, it is the perfect launchpad — wake up to fjord views, grab a walk along the sculpture-lined waterfront, and taxi to Oslo Central Station by 8:00 AM.

  • Astrup Fearnley Museum physically connected — free for guests
  • Sir Peter Blake originals and rotating contemporary collection
  • THIEF Spa with Turkish hamam, sauna, and pool
  • The Apparatjik Suite — interactive music/video art by a-ha's Magne Furuholmen
  • 2025 renovation has freshened the entire property
Recommended

Deluxe Room — East-Facing Fjord Views

For one night before the Bergen Railway departure, the fjord-facing Deluxe (~29-32 sqm) gives you the signature experience: wake up to Oslofjord views, coffee on the private balcony, golden morning light. No need to overspend on a suite you'll barely use.

Upgrade option: Junior Suite (66 sqm) if you want separate living space and a bathtub. The Oslo Suite (94 sqm + 160 sqm rooftop terrace) with Sir Peter Blake originals and private dining room is the crown jewel, but overkill for a single night.

Breakfast

Included in all room rates. Full Norwegian spread: smoked salmon, brown cheese (brunost), eggs, pastries, fresh fruit. Guests consistently rave about quality and variety. Plan for 6:30 AM start — you need to catch the Bergen Railway.

Bergen

Opus XVI

An Edvard Grieg Heritage Hotel · Small Luxury Hotels of the World
Address Vagsallmenningen 16, Bergen
Rooms 65 rooms & suites
Built 1876 (bank), hotel 2018
Member Small Luxury Hotels (SLH)
Correction from earlier plans

Opus XVI has 65 rooms in an 1876 bank building (Bergens Kreditbank), not 16 rooms in an 1861 stock exchange. The 1862 stock exchange is a separate hotel across the street (Bergen Bors Hotel, 127 rooms). The two properties are connected via an underground tunnel — you can dine at Bergen Bors's Michelin-starred restaurants regardless of where you stay.

Why This One

The only hotel in the world owned and operated by descendants of a famous composer. Edvard Grieg — Norway's most celebrated musician, born in Bergen in 1843 — is woven into every detail. The building's architect was Grieg's cousin. The hotel name comes from Grieg's Piano Concerto in A minor, Op. 16. An in-house exhibition tells his story through music, and during summer months, the hotel hosts live Grieg lunch concerts.

The 1876 bank building itself is magnificent: marble columns, stucco lustro walls, and polished granite from the original banking hall. This is not a generic luxury hotel — it is a deeply personal expression of Bergen's most important cultural figure, housed in one of its finest buildings.

  • TripAdvisor #1 hotel in Bergen (out of 53 hotels)
  • Edvard Grieg heritage in every room and public space
  • Summer Grieg concerts (June-August, bookable)
  • SLH membership guarantees boutique curation
  • Walking distance to Bryggen, Fish Market, Floyen funicular
  • Bergen Bors across the street for Michelin dining (Bare, 1 star) and Frescohallen (1920s murals, national treasure seafood hall)
Recommended

Deluxe Room or Mini-Suite

For three nights, room size matters. The Deluxe Room offers the best heritage features and views at a reasonable price point. The Mini-Suite adds a separate seating area and upgraded bathroom — worth it for a three-night stay. Note: rooms vary significantly in layout due to the historic building's irregular floor plan. Request a specific room type at booking.

The splurge: Two-Story Suite with private dining room — the most extraordinary accommodation in Bergen. Two levels of 1876 bank grandeur.

Don't miss — Bergen Bors dining

The Bergen Bors Hotel across the street has two must-visit restaurants: Bare (Bergen's first Michelin star, seasonal Nordic tasting menus ~EUR 200) and Frescohallen (spectacular triple-height seafood restaurant with restored 1920s murals by Alex Revold — a national treasure). Both are open to non-guests.

Price Estimate

$250 – $500
Per night for rooms · Suites $500-800+

Breakfast

Included in all room rates. Norwegian buffet plus a la carte cooked-to-order dishes. Guests rave about the quality, variety, and gluten-free options. Likely the best hotel breakfast in Bergen. Typically served 7:00-10:00 AM — confirm early service (6:30 AM) with the hotel at booking.

Nearby Breakfast Alternatives

  • Frescohallen at Bergen Bors — seafood breakfast in a triple-height painted hall
  • Godt Brod — Bergen's best artisan bakery, organic sourdough and pastries
  • Bergen Fish Market — fresh shrimp and smoked salmon at 7 AM

Nearby

  • Bryggen UNESCO wharf (5 min walk)
  • Fish Market (3 min walk)
  • Floyen funicular (8 min walk)
  • Bergen Bors / Bare Michelin (across the street)
  • KODE art museums (10 min walk)

Ett Hem

A Home · Ilse Crawford · Small Luxury Hotels of the World
Address Skogskloversvagen 2, Ostermalm, Stockholm
Rooms 22 rooms + 3 apartments
Built 1910 Arts & Crafts, hotel 2012
Designer Ilse Crawford / Studioilse
Correction from earlier plans

Ett Hem now has 22 rooms and 3 apartments, expanded from the original 12 rooms by acquiring two neighboring townhouses. Ilse Crawford designed all three residences. Still tiny by hotel standards — the intimate residential feel is fully preserved.

Why This One

"Ett Hem" means "A Home" in Swedish. This may be the world's most acclaimed residential-concept hotel. There is no reception desk — you are greeted at the door. There is no restaurant — you eat in the kitchen, where chefs prepare dinner while you sit at tables nearby. You drink in the sitting room, read in the library, and nap in the garden. Home-baked cakes are available all day — help yourself, like you would in a friend's impossibly elegant Stockholm townhouse.

Interior designer Ilse Crawford — one of the most celebrated in the world — created spaces that blend contemporary and vintage: cane, wood, leather, velvet in gold, brown, and cream. Michael Anastassiades lighting. Colored-glass ornaments. Brass cabinets. Every room is individually designed with her signature warm minimalism. The garden is a hidden green oasis: summer breakfasts al fresco, evening fire pits, glass houses with festival lights.

  • The kitchen IS the restaurant — farm-fresh, seasonal, cooked in front of you
  • The garden — fire pits, blankets, midnight sky in summer twilight
  • The library — floor-to-ceiling books, curl up by the fireplace
  • Susanne Kaufmann toiletries in every room
  • Sauna, spa, and fitness center on site
  • No two rooms alike — each one an Ilse Crawford original
Recommended

Split-Level Attic Suite — 43 sqm

The most romantic room type. Split-level layout under the eaves: downstairs has a sofa and cast-iron stove; upstairs is a wood-clad bedroom with king bed and in-room roll-top bathtub. For three nights at the world's most famous residential hotel, this is the one that gives you the full "living in a Stockholm townhouse" experience.

The ultimate: Attic Residence (70 sqm) with freestanding tub, fireplace, original ceiling decor, and writing desk. The largest and most atmospheric suite. Junior Suite with fireplace and/or balcony is also excellent if the Attic Suite is unavailable.

Price Estimate

$600 – $1,500
Per night · Budget $2,000-4,500 total for 3 nights

Breakfast

Included in all room rates. Fresh orange juice, yoghurt, fruits and berries, cheese, cold cuts, pastries, and cooked dishes. Served in the kitchen or conservatory. In summer: breakfast in the garden. The residential concept means flexible timing — confirm 7:00 AM start (may accommodate earlier requests).

Nearby Breakfast Alternatives

  • Ostermalms Saluhall — Stockholm's finest food hall, freshly renovated
  • Fabrique — Stockholm's best cinnamon buns (kanelbullar) and sourdough
  • Gateau — classic Swedish fika cafe near Humlegarden park

Nearby

Tallink Silja Line

Overnight ferry Stockholm → Helsinki · The crossing IS the destination
Ships Silja Serenade / Silja Symphony
Route Stockholm → Aland → Helsinki
Departs ~16:30 from Vartahamnen
Arrives ~09:30 at Olympia Terminal
Duration ~16h 45min
Updated recommendation

We recommend Tallink Silja over Viking Line. Key reasons: Silja has daily departures (Viking only sails every other day — if Jul 3 is not a Viking day, you are stuck). Silja's famous multi-story Promenade deck is unique to their ships. Silja has a more refined atmosphere — Viking has a reputation as the "party boat." The practical page has been updated accordingly.

Why This One

This is not just transport — this is a destination. You will sail through the Stockholm archipelago (30,000 islands) and the Finnish archipelago in perpetual summer twilight, with the sun barely dipping below the horizon. The Silja ships feature a famous multi-story indoor Promenade — a full pedestrian street with shops, bars, and restaurants running the length of the vessel. There is a massive Scandinavian smorgasbord buffet (100+ dishes), a sauna onboard, tax-free shopping, live music, and a champagne bar.

For ultimate luxury: Premium Suite (75 sqm) on Deck 11 if available. Or one of the Themed Premium Cabins (42-49 sqm) on Deck 9 — Spring, Summer, or Autumn themed.

Hotel St. George

Design Hotels · The only permanent Ai Weiwei hotel installation in the world
Address Yrjonkatu 13, Helsinki
Rooms 148 rooms + 5 suites
Built 1840 (printing house), hotel 2018
Member Design Hotels (Marriott Bonvoy)
Updated recommendation

We recommend Hotel St. George over Klaus K for Helsinki. While Klaus K's Kalevala theme is culturally rich, St. George offers a more compelling "impossible to replicate in NYC" experience: the only permanent Ai Weiwei hotel installation worldwide, 400+ artworks, a full spa with pool (critical after a night on a ferry), and an outstanding breakfast that matches this couple's priorities.

Why This One

Walk into the lobby and look up: suspended above you is Tianwu, a massive bamboo-and-silk polycephalic dragon by Ai Weiwei. It is the only Ai Weiwei artwork displayed permanently in a public hotel space anywhere in the world. That alone makes this a destination. But the hotel goes deeper — 400+ pieces of art are spread across seven floors of a former 19th-century printing house and artists' studio, transformed with Nordic elegance.

The rooms are bathed in natural light with soft mint green, pearl grey, and brown tones. Handcrafted furniture meets state-of-the-art technology. The bathrooms are extraordinary: spacious, double sinks, soaking tubs, Japanese-style bidet toilets, heated floors. After a night on the Silja ferry, the St. George Care Spa — with indoor pool, two saunas, cold plunge pool, and a Light Cognitive light wall — is exactly what you need.

  • Ai Weiwei's "Tianwu" — the only permanent hotel Ai Weiwei in the world
  • 400+ artworks across seven floors
  • St. George Care Spa — pool, two saunas, cold plunge, light therapy wall
  • Restaurant Boon Nam by Chef Tomi Bjorck — a love letter to Thai cuisine
  • The Wintergarden — one of Helsinki's most atmospheric cocktail spots
  • The Bakery — excellent coffee and pastries
  • Overlooks Old Church Park
Recommended

Deluxe Room or Junior Suite

The Deluxe Rooms offer the best combination of natural light, art integration, and those extraordinary bathrooms (soaking tub, heated floors, double sinks). For two nights, the space is comfortable and the design is world-class. A Junior Suite adds a separate living area for unwinding after the ferry crossing.

For a statement: One of the 5 named suites for the full art-hotel experience — each is individually curated.

Post-ferry recovery plan

You arrive from the Silja ferry at ~09:30 AM. Drop bags at St. George (early check-in request recommended), then head straight to the spa: swim, sauna, cold plunge. Follow with the outstanding breakfast buffet (fresh bread brought to table, premium quality). You will feel human again by noon.

46 rooms · SLH Member · $350-600/night

Helsinki

Waldorf Astoria Helsinki

116 rooms + 32 suites · Reflagged Oct 2025 · $280-600/night

First Waldorf Astoria in the Nordics. 1885-1930 interconnected townhouses (former officers' barracks). Select suites include private saunas, steam rooms, and jacuzzis on private terraces. Peacock Bar. Spa under glass-roof atrium. The Emir of Qatar reserved the entire hotel during his 2024 state visit.

Why not chosen: Hilton corporate chain may feel less "authentic boutique." But the private sauna suites are extraordinary — strong runner-up.
Helsinki

Klaus K Hotel

170 rooms · Since 1938/2005 · Preferred Hotels · $200-400/night

Design drawn from the Kalevala, Finland's national epic. Four room categories named after Kalevala emotions (Passion, Mystical, Desire, Envy). Standout suite with egg-shaped bed inspired by the creation myth. Sky Terrace Rooftop Bar. Custom beds by Unikulma.

Why not chosen: 170 rooms = not boutique. Some noise issues. Less luxurious than St. George. But the Kalevala theme IS uniquely Finnish — best budget alternative.
Helsinki

Hotel Kamp

179 rooms · Since 1887 · Leading Hotels of the World · $350-600/night

Helsinki's most prestigious hotel. Where Sibelius socialized with European aristocracy. Opposite Esplanade Park. Currently undergoing major 2023-2026 renovation (new wing, new entrance, all rooms refreshed).

Why not chosen: Renovation ongoing through 2026 — spa closed, possible construction impact. Classic grand hotel style is replicable in NYC (think The Plaza).
Bergen

Bergen Bors Hotel

127 rooms · 1862 stock exchange · Claesson Koivisto Rune design · $200-500/night

Neo-Renaissance former stock exchange across the street from Opus XVI (connected by underground tunnel). Home to Bare (Michelin star), Frescohallen (spectacular 1920s mural seafood hall), and Handelskammeret Bar (best cocktails in Bergen).

Why not chosen: Opus XVI's Edvard Grieg heritage and SLH membership are more aligned with the trip profile. But dine at Bergen Bors regardless.
