Landet Stay

Architect cabin in a 400-hectare nature reserve, Sörmland coast. Activities & food within driving distance.
Jul 2–3 · 2 nights
The Essential Five
Ruthlessly prioritized. If you do nothing else, do these.
Stendörren Nature Reserve Not in NYC
Nature 20–30 min drive · Free
Suspension bridges connecting rocky Baltic islands. Walk past where casual visitors stop — keep going to the outer rocks. Swim off warm granite into clear water. Bring bakery coffee and pastries for breakfast on the cliffs. Best before 8 AM or at golden hour (~8–10 PM).
Go early or at sunset. The pine-scented silence + warm rock + cold water is peak Sweden.
Trosa — Harbor Grazing Not in NYC
Food 20–30 min drive
Tiny harbor town called “the end of the world.” Don’t do one formal meal — graze. Bakery for cardamom buns at 7 AM, harbor for smoked fish, roadside strawberry stands. Come back at 9 PM for soft light and ice cream on the quay. Fina Fisken (seafood) or Trosa Sjökrog (waterfront grill) for dinner — book ahead.
Best consumed as a sequence of small bites, not a destination restaurant.
James Turrell Skyspace, Ytterjärna Not in NYC
Art 40–50 min drive · 260 SEK/pp · Must book
Scandinavia’s first permanent Turrell installation. Book a sunset session — at this latitude in July, sunset is ~10 PM and the sky barely darkens. The world’s northernmost Skyspace creates light effects impossible anywhere else.
skyspace.se →
Kayaking in the Trosa Archipelago Not in NYC
Nature 20–30 min to Trosa · ~500 SEK half-day rental
Morning rental, 2–4 hours. Protected water, reed beds, little red boathouses, skerries. Pack bakery breakfast. Ask for a route with birdlife and an outer-rock swim stop. Calmer and less performative than Stockholm archipelago paddling.
Farm-Stand Crawl on Back Roads Not in NYC
Food Everywhere · Free–cheap
Early July = peak Swedish strawberries, new potatoes, eggs, local honey. If you see a handwritten sign for jordgubbar (strawberries) or gårdsbutik (farm shop), stop. Honor-system Swish payment. Bergs Gård near Västerljung has a proper farm shop (honey, apple juice, marmalade) — 25–35 min drive.
Swedish summer produce from roadside stands is quietly one of the most luxurious parts of the trip.
Nature & Water
Sävö Island Not in NYC
Nature Boat from Trosa · ~200 SEK RT
Car-free archipelago island. Meadows, birdlife, public sauna. Half-day slow island walk — bring a picnic. Check summer boat schedule from Trosa harbor.
Sörmlandsleden Hiking Trails
Nature 15–60 min to trailheads · Free
Sweden’s longest regional trail — 1,000+ km. Pick a coastal or forest segment, 1.5–3 hrs. Bergsrundan loop near Trosa is closest. Ancient Swedish forest, granite outcrops, wild blueberry patches. 18 hours of daylight.
Ask Landet Stay which section near them is actually beautiful, not just convenient.
Forest Lake Swimming (Badplatser)
Nature 20–45 min drive · Free
One sea swim + one inland lake swim = the full Sörmland spectrum. Dark water, warm shallows, dragonflies, pine silence. Ask Landet for their favorite nearby klippbad (rock swimming spot) or badplats.
Öster Malma Wildlife Park
Nature 40–50 min drive · ~150 SEK
Manor estate with guaranteed moose, red deer, fallow deer in natural forest. Run by the Swedish Hunters’ Association. Also has canoeing and forest walks. Good “wildlife safari lite” if Landet’s own reserve doesn’t deliver sightings.
Wild Berry Foraging Not in NYC
Nature On the property · Free
Allemansrätten (Right of Public Access) means you can pick freely. Early July = wild strawberries and early blueberries. Walk into the forest at dawn. The pure sensory opposite of Manhattan.
Sauna + Baltic Dip at Sunset Not in NYC
Unique On the property · Included
Landet has its own sauna. Heat up, plunge into the Baltic (~18–20°C in July), repeat. At 10 PM in the endless Nordic twilight. The hot-cold cycle is deeply restorative and quintessentially Swedish.
Food & Drink
Bergs Gård Nature Table Not in NYC
Food Experience 25–35 min · ~600 SEK/pp · Must book
A farm where you pick up a basket of local ingredients, walk 1 km through an oak garden trail to an outdoor “nature table” overlooking ponds and pastures, and cook your own Swedish farm meal outdoors. Also has a farm shop with local honey and preserves.
bergsgard.se →
Gripsholms Värdshus, Mariefred
Food 50–60 min drive · Book ahead
One of Sweden’s oldest inns, right on Lake Mälaren next to Gripsholm Castle. Classic Swedish cuisine, atmospheric dining room. Perfect for a special lunch combined with the castle visit.
Strawberry Self-Picking Farms
Food Various · ~50 SEK/kg
Early July = peak Swedish strawberry season. Small, intensely sweet, sun-warm. Picking your own in a Sörmland field at golden hour is as Swedish summer as it gets. Check visitsormland.se for locations.
Loppis (Flea Markets)
Culture Roadside · Varies
Look for signs saying loppis, antik, or retro. Barn sales with old Swedish glass, pine furniture, enamelware, woven textiles. The best design experience in rural Sweden is often a half-chaotic summer flea market in a barn.
Culture & Castles
Gripsholm Castle, Mariefred
Culture 50–60 min drive · 170 SEK
16th-century royal castle on Lake Mälaren. Swedish National Portrait Gallery spanning 500 years. Town of Mariefred is impossibly charming — pastel wooden houses, lakefront promenades. Heritage steam train from Läggesta in summer. Combine with fika at Annas Hembageri.
Taxinge Slott — The Cake Castle
Food Culture 60–70 min drive · ~60 SEK/slice
65 varieties of homemade cake in a manor house. Peak Swedish fika taken to its logical extreme. Beautiful castle grounds for a post-cake walk. A beloved local institution, not a tourist trap. Daily 11–18 in summer.
Tullgarn Palace Grounds
Culture Nature 20–40 min drive · Free grounds
Royal summer residence with grounds spilling into the coast. Giant trees, grazed land, old paths, water access. Less formal than Drottningholm, more intimate. Go early for the landscape, not the interior tour.
Julita Manor Gardens
Culture Nature 50–60 min drive · Free (park)
Grand estate with one of Sweden’s largest peony collections. Heritage gardens, kitchen garden, rose garden, orchard. Early July = peak peony and rose season. Run by Nordiska Museet.
Suggested 2 Days

Day 1 (Jul 2): Arrive + Coast + Trosa

Morning
Fly Bergen → Stockholm. Pick up rental car. Drive to Landet (~1 hr south).
~1 PM
Arrive. First walk through the 400-hectare reserve. Settle into the cabin.
Late Afternoon
Drive to Trosa (~20 min). Harbor grazing — bakery, smoked fish, strawberry stands.
Evening
Dinner at Fina Fisken or Trosa Sjökrog (book ahead). Ice cream on the quay at 9 PM.
~10 PM
Back to cabin. Sauna + Baltic dip in golden twilight. Fire pit. Silence.

Day 2 (Jul 3): Full Sörmland Day

6:30 AM
Early swim off rocks near the cabin. Wild berry foraging on the property.
Morning
Stendörren Nature Reserve — suspension bridges, outer rocks, coffee on the cliffs. Swim off warm granite.
Midday
Kayak in Trosa archipelago (2–4 hrs) OR farm-stand crawl on back roads.
Afternoon
Rest at cabin. Read. Nap. Explore further trails or the Sörmland coast.
Evening
James Turrell Skyspace sunset session at ~10 PM (if booked) OR fire pit + white-night stillness at the cabin.

• “Where would you take a friend from abroad to show them real Sörmland in 2 hours?”
